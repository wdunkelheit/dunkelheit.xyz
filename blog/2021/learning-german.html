Learning German

I have decided to learn German. I have always had an interest in learning another language but due to the terrible methodology used by the education system and cringe meme apps like DuoLingo I was never motivated enough to stick them out. However I recently came to discover the Michel Thomas method after reading these articles by Luke Smith: Notes on Learning Languages and Learning European Languages (Michel Thomas). I won’t bother to sumamrize them here but you should definetely read them if you are interested in learning a language.

After reading these articles I was convinced to give the method a try. I will update this post every so often with my thoughts on the method so far.

So why German?

It’s probably the most relevant language for me to learn based on its similarities to English, my musical interests and the location of a lot of my friends. I would love to be able to travel there without being a “that guy” tourist that just speaks in English the whole time.

Additionally I believe that learning German will give me reason to explore other related languages. I have always found languages interesting, who knows maybe one day I will be able to speak to people on every continent? I’d also like to put the theory that thinking in multiple languages can give you a better understanding of concepts to the test.
